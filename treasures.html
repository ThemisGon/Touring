Treasures

Are you ready to unveal the tresures of Andros?

Hidden in the heart of the Aegean lies an island of quiet grace and timeless spirit Andros. For those who walk its winding paths and stone-paved streets, the island does not merely show its beauty. It whispers its stories.

The Statue Of Latent Seaman

One such story stands tall at the edge of Chora: the Statue of the Unknown Sailor. Facing the sea with stoic silence, the bronze figure honors all those lost to the waves, nameless yet never forgotten. It is not just a monument, but a sentinel—guarding the memory of sacrifice.

The Bridge of Love

Not far from there, tucked beneath trees and embraced by nature, is the Bridge of Love. An old stone arch reaching over a dry riverbed, it carries the name of lovers past and present. Couples still visit it, making wishes or promises beneath its arch, as if the stones remember every whispered vow.

Old Woman's Beach the jump

During the Ottoman occupation, legend has it that the conquerors were unable to seize the fortress that stood proud on the island. Desperate, they turned to deceit. An old woman and her pregnant daughter approached the fortress, pleading for refuge. The locals, moved by their plight, allowed them inside. But as night fell, treachery struck. The old woman secretly opened a gate, allowing the Turkish forces to storm the fortress. The invaders massacred the people who had sought protection within its walls. Overcome with shame and regret for the devastation she had caused, the old woman climbed a towering cliff and, in despair, leapt into the sea. According to the locals, the very rock where she fell turned to stone. Today, at the beach known as Grias to Pidima — The Old Woman’s Leap — a striking, towering rock stands by the shore. From a certain angle, it eerily resembles the profile of an old woman wearing her traditional headscarf, forever frozen in time.
