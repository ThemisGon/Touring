Paths

Why does Andros have paths?

Andros has a rich network of footpaths, many of which are paved with stone. This network historically connected the island’s settlements with places of worship and its ports. Since the mid-20th century, as cars gradually became dominant, the use of these paths declined. In many cases, the old paths were either cut off or replaced by roads for vehicles. Today, Andros still maintains an extensive network of hiking trails, estimated to exceed 300 kilometers in total length. The accessible part of the trail network currently amounts to around 200 kilometers.Scattered throughout the island are over 200 recorded watermills, 69 windmills, numerous bridges (several of which are arched), and hundreds of country chapels.



What kind of Paths exist?

Depending on what you're looking for, Andros offers a plethora of paths to explore. Moreover, Andros is a unique island of the Cyclades, characterized by abundant water resources, a mountainous nature, rugged terrain, and an exceptionally diverse landscape. Small forests, fertile valleys, numerous beaches, year-round streams, waterfalls, and springs can be found on almost every slope and valley. The island’s continuous human presence has left visible traces even on its steepest slopes, shaping (primarily with stone) a landscape that is largely handmade. The variety of rural architecture is striking: dry-stone walls, terraces, olive presses, and threshing floors.

Into the path...

While following the path, these signs are placed along the trails to help guide hikers and ensure they stay on the correct path. Each sign provides direction, distance, and trail connection information. In case you lose your way or feel uncertain, following these signs will safely lead you to your destination or the next landmark on the route.
